# Legal & Ethics in Security Architecture

## 1. Orientation

### What this module covers
This module covers **The Law**. What you *can* do vs what you *should* do.

### Fit in the Learning Path
With great power comes great liability.

### Learning Objectives
By the end of this module, you should understand:
*   **Hack Back**. (Illegal).
*   **Employee Monitoring**. (Privacy).
*   **Code of Ethics**.

---

## 2. Core Content

### Hack Back
You find the attacker's C2 server. You have an exploit for it.
*   **Action**: Do you hack them to delete your data?
*   **Law**: NO. Computer Fraud and Abuse Act (CFAA) makes this a felony. You are not Batman.

### Monitoring
*   Can you read employee email?
*   *US*: Generally Yes (Company Asset).
*   *EU*: Generally No (Human Right to Privacy).
*   *Architect*: Know your jurisdiction.

---

## 3. Guided Practice

### The Whistleblower
**Scenario**: You find the company is selling user data illegally.
**Ethics**:
1.  Report internally (Compliance Officer).
2.  Report extensively (Regulator).
*   Check Whistleblower protections.

---

## 4. Reflection Check

1.  **CISO Liability**: CISOs are personally being charged for covering up breaches (e.g., Uber case).
2.  **Transparency**: Don't lie to regulators. It turns a fine into a jail sentence.
3.  **Ethics**: ISC2 Code of Ethics: "Protect society, the common good, necessary public trust and confidence, and the infrastructure."

---

## 5. Completion Criteria

This module is complete when:
1.  You vow **Never to Hack Back**.
2.  You understand **Jurisdictional Differences**.
3.  You adhere to a professional **Code of Ethics**.
