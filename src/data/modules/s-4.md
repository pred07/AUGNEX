# Adversary Mindset & Psychology

## 1. Orientation

### What this module covers
Sun Tzu: "Know your enemy." To catch a hacker, you must think like one. This module explores the **economics, psychology, and structure** of threat groups. We move beyond "evil hackers" to understand them as **rational actors** running a business or military operation.

### Learning Objectives
*   Profile adversaries by **Motivation** (Cybercrime vs Espionage vs Hacktivism).
*   Understand the **Cybercrime Supply Chain** (Access Brokers, Affiliates, Devs).
*   Analyze **Rational Choice Theory** in target selection.

---

## 2. Core Content

### The Motivation Matrix

1.  **Cybercrime (eCrime)**
    *   *Goal:* Money.
    *   *Mindset:* ROI (Return on Investment). "Path of least resistance."
    *   *Behavior:* Quick smash-and-grab OR long-term ransomware extortion. They don't care who you are, only what you are worth.

2.  **Espionage (APT - Advanced Persistent Threat)**
    *   *Goal:* Information / State advantage.
    *   *Mindset:* Mission-oriented. "Persist until success."
    *   *Behavior:* Low and slow. Avoid detection at all costs. Targeted.

3.  **Hacktivism**
    *   *Goal:* Attention / Chaos / Political statement.
    *   *Mindset:* Ideological.
    *   *Behavior:* DDoS, Defacement, Doxxing. Loud and public.

4.  **Insider Threat**
    *   *Goal:* Revenge / Profit / Coercion.
    *   *Mindset:* Betrayal / Entitlement.
    *   *Behavior:* Uses legitimate access maliciously. Hardest to detect.

### The Cybercrime Economy (Ransomware-as-a-Service)
Modern cybercrime is a corporate ecosystem.
*   **Operators (The Brand):** Write the Ransomware (LockBit). Run the negotiation site. Take 20%.
*   **Affiliates (The Hackers):** Hack the victim. Deploy the ransomware. Take 80%.
*   **Access Brokers (IABs):** Hack a VPN, sell the credentials to the Affiliate for $2k.
*   **Result:** The person hacking you probably didn't write the malware. They are a contractor.

### Rational Choice Theory
Adversaries make cost-benefit analyses.
*   *Cost:* Time, Zero-day exploits (expensive), Risk of arrest.
*   *Benefit:* Payout, Data.
*   *Defense Strategy:* **Raise the Cost.** If you make it expensive to hack you (MFA, Segmentation), the Cybercriminal goes to the next target. The APT might keep trying, but you burn their budget.

## 3. Lab: Profiling the Actor

### Scenario
An attacker encrypted 50 servers and demanded $1M. They posted a sample of data on a "Wall of Shame."

### Analysis
*   **Motivation:** Financial (Ransom).
*   **Type:** eCrime (Ransomware).
*   **Structure:** Likely RaaS (Ransomware-as-a-Service).
*   **Prediction:** They will negotiate. They prioritize payment reliability.
*   **Action:** Check if there is a decryptor available? (No). Check if they are sanctioned? (OFAC check - essential for legal).
