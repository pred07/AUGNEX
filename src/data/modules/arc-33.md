# Dealing with Law Enforcement

## 1. Orientation

### What this module covers
This module covers **FBI / Interpol**. When to call them. When not to.

### Fit in the Learning Path
You are a civilian. They are the police. Know the boundary.

### Learning Objectives
By the end of this module, you should understand:
*   **Proactive Relationships**.
*   **Chain of Custody**.
*   **Subpoenas vs Warrants**.

---

## 2. Core Content

### To Call or Not to Call?
*   **Call**: Ransomware (for stats), Theft of Trade Secrets, Child Exploitation (Mandatory).
*   **Don't Call**: Minor internal policy violation.
*   *Risk*: Once you call, it's a crime scene. They might seize your servers. (Business stops).

### Relationship
Join **InfraGard** (FBI Partnership). Meet them *before* the crisis.

---

## 3. Guided Practice

### Evidence Handling
**Scenario**: You find a hacker's tool on a server.
**Action**:
1.  **Do not touch it**. (Timestamps matter).
2.  **Image the Industry**. (Forensic copy).
3.  **Hash the Image**. (Integrity).
4.  **Log Access**. (Chain of Custody).
*   If you tamper with it, it's inadmissible in court.

---

## 4. Reflection Check

1.  **Control**: You lose control of the investigation when LE gets involved.
2.  **Reciprocity**: Don't expect them to hack back for you. They won't. They want attribution.
3.  **Legal**: Always have General Counsel present when talking to LE.

---

## 5. Completion Criteria

This module is complete when:
1.  You have a contact at the **FBI/Local Cyber Unit**.
2.  You understand **Chain of Custody**.
3.  You know the difference between a **Warrant** and a **Subpoena**.
