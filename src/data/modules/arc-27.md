# Budgeting & ROI

## 1. Orientation

### What this module covers
This module covers **Money**. CAPEX vs OPEX.

### Fit in the Learning Path
You can't buy security with good intentions.

### Learning Objectives
By the end of this module, you should understand:
*   **CAPEX (Capital Ex)** vs **OPEX (Operational Ex)**.
*   **TCO (Total Cost of Ownership)**.
*   **Building a Business Case**.

---

## 2. Core Content

### The Ask
*   *Bad*: "I need $100k for a firewall."
*   *Good*: "We are currently losing $50k/year in downtime and face a $1M risk of breach. For an investment of $100k, we mitigate this risk."

### Hidden Costs
*   **Shelfware**: Buying a tool and not having staff to run it.
*   *Rule*: For every $1 spent on tools, spend $3 on people/training.

---

## 3. Guided Practice

### The Spreadsheet
**Scenario**: Choosing an EDR.
*   **Option A**: $50/endpoint. Agent only.
*   **Option B**: $80/endpoint. Managed Service (MDR).
*   **Analysis**: Option A requires hiring 2 analysts ($200k). Option B includes analysts.
*   **Conclusion**: Option B is cheaper TCO.

---

## 4. Reflection Check

1.  **Expiration**: Spend the budget or lose it. (End of Year flush).
2.  **Alignment**: Tie specific line items to specific Board goals ("Customer Trust Initiative").
3.  **Audit**: Be ready to prove you used the money well.

---

## 5. Completion Criteria

This module is complete when:
1.  You can explain **TCO**.
2.  You budget for **People**, not just Tools.
3.  You speak **Finance** (ROI, NPV).
