# Scaling the Purple Team

## 1. Orientation

### What this module covers
This module covers **Ad-Hoc** to **Dedicated** teams.

### Fit in the Learning Path
You started with one person. Now you need a program.

### Learning Objectives
By the end of this module, you should understand:
*   **Matrix Teams**.
*   **Rotation Programs**.
*   **Champions**.

---

## 2. Core Content

### Models
1.  **Ad-Hoc**: Red and Blue meet once a month. (Good start).
2.  **Matrix**: Specific members form a virtual "Purple Squad" for a sprint.
3.  **Dedicated**: Full-time Purple Teamers who do nothing but Detection Engineering and Emulation.

### Rotations
*   Send a Defender to the Red Team for 3 months.
*   Send an Attacker to the SOC for 3 months.
*   *Result*: Instant empathy and skill transfer.

---

## 3. Guided Practice

### The "Security Champion"
**Concept**: A developer in a product team who loves security.
**Action**: Train them in Purple concepts.
**Result**: They fix the bugs before the code even reaches you.

**Task**: Identify one person in your org who could be a Champion.

---

## 4. Reflection Check

1.  **Burnout**: Continuous testing is exhausting. Rotation helps.
2.  **Career Path**: Purple Teaming is a great stepping stone to CISO.
3.  **Budget**: It's cheaper to train existing staff than to hire unicorns.

---

## 5. Completion Criteria

This module is complete when:
1.  You identify a **Scaling Model**.
2.  You advocate for **Job Rotations**.
3.  You empower **Security Champions**.
