# Corporate & Business Intelligence

## 1. Orientation

### What this module covers
Investigating companies, not just networks. Finding ownership structures, bankruptcy filings, shell companies, and supply chain dependencies. This is critical for **Supply Chain Risk Management (SCRM)**.

### Learning Objectives
*   Map **Corporate Hierarchies** and Parents/Subsidiaries.
*   Investigate **Ultimate Beneficial Owners (UBOs)**.
*   Analyze **Supply Chain Risk**.

---

## 2. Core Content

### 1. Business Registers
Every legal company must register.
*   **OpenCorporates:** The largest open database of companies.
*   **SEC EDGAR (USA):** 10-K filings for public companies. Reveals risks, subsidiaries, executive pay.
*   **Companies House (UK):** Directors, insolvency history. PO Box addresses.

### 2. The Supply Chain Map
Targeting a bank is hard. Targeting their HVAC vendor is easy (Target 2013 Breach).
*   **Method:** Look for "Success Stories" or "Case Studies" on vendor websites. "We secure Acme Corp!" -> Intel.
*   **Technique:** Import/Export records (Panjiva, ImportGenius) show who ships servers to whom.

### 3. Bankruptcy & Litigation
*   A company in financial distress cuts corners on security.
*   **Pacer (USA):** Court records. Lawsuits reveal internal dysfunction and specific technology disputes.

## 3. Lab: Supply Chain Audit
**Task:** Identify the Software Vendors of "Bank X".
1.  **Job Boards:** "Hiring React Developer", "Experience with AWS preferred".
2.  **LinkedIn:** Employees listing "Salesforce Administrator" as their title.
3.  **Vendor Sites:** Search specific vendors for "Bank X" in their customer list.
4.  **Risk:** If Salesforce goes down, Bank X CRM goes down.
